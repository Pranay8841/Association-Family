CHARTERED ACCOUNTANT (CA)

Chartered Accountancy course is one of the best course after passing Class 12th Examination. To become a Chartered Accountant in India, you have to complete Chartered Accountancy Course. The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) regulates this course.

About ICAI: The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) is a statutory body set up by the Chartered Accountants Act, 1949. ICAI is the second largest professional body of Chartered Accountants in the world. Initially it has only 1,700 members in 1949 and today the membership of the CA Institute is over 3,10,000. There are more than 6,00,000 students are pursuing chartered accountancy course in 2022.

CHARTERED ACCOUNTANT 2024 Study Plan, Preparation Strategy

This is a preparation guide for CHARTERED ACCOUNTANT 2024 aspirants. Here we will cover CHARTERED ACCOUNTANT preparation tips, strategy, study plan and all other important things to keep in mind for the CHARTERED ACCOUNTANT 2024 examination.

There are three levels in this course viz. CA Foundation , CA Intermediate and CA Final. Under newly revised Scheme of Education and Training, a candidate can pursue Chartered Accountancy Course either through Foundation Course Route or through Direct Entry Route.
Early registration to CA Foundation Course is available for 10th class passed candidates. Otherwise, graduation degree is the eligibility criteria for direct admission to CA Intermediate Course.
Total duration of CA Course is 4.5 years out of which 3 years is for practical training i.e. Articleship and 4 months and 8 months is for study period in CA Foundation and Intermediate .
Before joining CA course, you should have knowledge about duration of Chartered Accountancy course.

Explaining the CA course duration in detail here under:

Become CA through Foundation Course Route:

CA Foundation course duration is 8 months; Study Period 4 Months Exam Period, 1 month Time to declare CA Foundation result. 2 months Time required for Admission to CA Intermediate Course is 1 month. CA Intermediate course duration is 14 months; Study Period 8 Months, Exam Period 1 month. Time to declare CA Inter result 2 months. Time required for Admission to CA Final Course is 1 month, CA Final course duration is 36 months, that requires to complete 3 years articleship.

If you are graduate then you may become CA in just 3 years. Direct entry is open for commerce graduates or post-graduates ( 55% ) or other graduates or post-graduates (60%) :

Step-1: CA Foundation Course: It is the entry-level course. Candidates who wish to pursue Chartered Accountancy Course can register for Foundation Course after passing in Class 12th examination. Following are papers of CA Foundation Course:

  • Paper-1: Principles and Practice of Accounting.
  • Paper-2: Business Laws and Business Correspondence and Reporting.
  • Paper-3: Business Mathematics and Logical Reasoning & Statistics.
  • Paper-4: Business Economics and Business and Commercial Knowledge.

Step-2: CA Intermediate Course : Candidates can register for Intermediate level after qualifying Foundation Course. You may take direct entry, if you are Graduate or have qualified the Intermediate Level examination of CMA Institute or CS Institute. Four weeks ICITSS and three years practical training required. There are two Groups in CA Intermediate level viz. Group-I (Paper-1 to Paper-4) and Group-II (Paper-5 to Paper-7).
Following subjects are of CA Intermediate Course:

  • Paper-1: Accounting.
  • Paper-2: Corporate and Other Laws.
  • Paper-3: Cost and Management Accounting.
  • Paper-4: Taxation.
  • Paper-5: Advanced Accounting.
  • Paper-6: Auditing and Assurance.
  • Paper-7: Enterprise Information Systems & Strategic Management.
  • Paper-8: Financial Management & Economics for Finance.

Step-3: CA Final Course : After successfully passing Intermediate Examination, a student enters into the last leg of the Chartered Accountancy Course i.e. the Final Course. There are two Groups in CA Final level viz. Group-I (Paper-1 to Paper-4) and Group-II (Paper-5 to Paper-8).
Following subjects cover entire syllabus of CA Final course:

  • Paper-1: Financial Reporting.
  • Paper-2: Strategic Financial Management.
  • Paper-3: Advanced Auditing and Professional Ethics.
  • Paper-4: Corporate and Economic Laws.
  • Paper-5: Strategic Cost Management and Performance Evaluation.
  • Paper-6: Elective Subjects.
  • Paper-6A: Risk Management.
  • Paper-6B: Financial Services and Capital Markets.
  • Paper-6C: International Taxation.
  • Paper-6D: Economic Laws.
  • Paper-6E: Global Financial Reporting Standards.
  • Paper-6F: Multidisciplinary Case Study.
  • Paper-7: Enterprise Information Systems & Strategic Management.
  • Paper-8: Financial Management & Economics for Finance.

After passing Final course, you will be a qualified Chartered Accountant and eligible to submit application in Form No. 2 for entry in the register of members of ICAI.

Enrol as a member of the ICAI and be designated as “ Chartered Accountant”.
